ACME CORPORATION — EMPLOYEE POLICIES HANDBOOK
Version 3.2 | Effective January 2025
==============================================

SECTION 1: REMOTE WORK POLICY
------------------------------

Eligibility
Employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period and whose role does not require
on-site presence are eligible to work remotely. Eligibility is determined by the employee's
direct manager in coordination with HR.

Remote Work Days
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week. The specific days must be agreed upon with
the direct manager and documented in writing at the start of each quarter. Core collaboration
days (typically Tuesday and Thursday) require in-office presence unless a formal exception is
approved by the department head.

Home Office Requirements
Employees working remotely must maintain a dedicated, distraction-free workspace with:
- Reliable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps download / 10 Mbps upload)
- A locked cabinet or encrypted storage for any physical documents
- A company-issued or approved device with up-to-date security software

Equipment and Expenses
The company provides a one-time home office stipend of $800 for eligible employees. Monthly
internet reimbursement of up to $50 is available upon submission of receipts. Personal devices
used for work must be enrolled in the company MDM solution.

Availability and Responsiveness
Remote employees must be reachable during core hours (9:00 AM – 5:00 PM local time) via Slack
and email. Video camera must be enabled during all team meetings unless a technical issue is
reported to IT in advance.

Performance and Accountability
Remote work is a privilege contingent on sustained performance. Managers may revoke remote work
eligibility with 2 weeks' notice if performance or collaboration issues arise.

SECTION 2: LEAVE POLICY
------------------------

Annual Leave
Full-time employees accrue 15 days of paid annual leave per year, increasing to 20 days after
3 years of service and 25 days after 7 years. Leave must be requested in the company HR portal
at least 5 business days in advance for periods of 3 or more consecutive days.

Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per calendar year. Sick leave does not
carry over to the following year. For absences longer than 3 consecutive days, a medical
certificate from a licensed physician is required.

Parental Leave
Primary caregivers: 16 weeks fully paid parental leave, commencing from the birth or adoption
date. Secondary caregivers: 4 weeks fully paid parental leave. Leave may be taken in one
continuous block or split into two periods within the first 12 months.

Public Holidays
Employees observe all public holidays in their country of employment. Remote employees in
countries with different public holiday schedules follow the schedule of their country of
residence.

Unpaid Leave
Extended unpaid leave of up to 12 weeks may be granted for exceptional personal circumstances,
subject to manager and HR approval. Benefits (health insurance, pension contributions) continue
during unpaid leave for periods up to 4 weeks.

SECTION 3: EXPENSE POLICY
--------------------------

Approval Thresholds
- Under $50: No pre-approval required; submit within 30 days with receipt.
- $50 – $500: Manager approval required before expense is incurred.
- Over $500: Department head approval required. Finance team must be notified.

Travel Expenses
Economy class flights are the default for all business travel. Business class is permitted for
flights exceeding 8 hours or where the employee travels overnight. Hotel bookings should not
exceed the city rate cap published quarterly by Finance (available on the intranet).

Meal Allowance
$75 per day for domestic travel. $100 per day for international travel. Alcohol is reimbursable
only at client entertainment events with prior manager approval.

Non-Reimbursable Items
The following are not reimbursable: personal entertainment, mini-bar charges, traffic fines,
gym fees, and any expense not directly related to business activity.

Submission Deadline
All expense reports must be submitted within 45 days of the expense being incurred. Late
submissions require CFO approval and may result in non-reimbursement.

SECTION 4: CODE OF CONDUCT
---------------------------

Respect and Inclusion
ACME is committed to maintaining a workplace free from discrimination, harassment, and bullying.
All employees are expected to treat colleagues, clients, and partners with dignity and respect,
regardless of race, gender, age, religion, disability, sexual orientation, or national origin.

Conflicts of Interest
Employees must disclose any financial interest or secondary employment that could conflict with
ACME's interests. Disclosure forms are submitted annually to HR and whenever a new potential
conflict arises.

Confidentiality
Employees must not disclose proprietary information, client data, trade secrets, or strategic
plans to third parties without written authorization. This obligation survives employment
termination for a period of 2 years.

Use of Company Resources
Company devices, networks, and software licenses are for business use. Incidental personal use
is acceptable provided it does not compromise security, violate any policy, or create liability
for the company.

Social Media
Employees must not post content that disparages ACME, its clients, or colleagues. Personal
opinions shared publicly must be clearly identified as personal views and not those of the company.

Reporting Violations
Employees who observe a potential policy violation should report it to their manager, HR, or
through the anonymous Ethics Hotline: ethics@acme-corp.com or +1-800-ACME-ETH.

Disciplinary Process
Violations of this Code may result in a written warning, suspension, or termination, depending
on severity. All investigations are conducted confidentially by HR.

SECTION 5: DATA HANDLING POLICY
---------------------------------

Data Classification
ACME classifies data into four tiers:
- Public: Marketing materials, press releases.
- Internal: General business communications, meeting notes.
- Confidential: Client contracts, financial reports, HR records.
- Restricted: Personal Identifiable Information (PII), payment data, trade secrets.

Handling Requirements
Confidential and Restricted data must be stored only in approved, encrypted systems. Transfer
of Restricted data outside the company network requires VPN and encryption. Physical documents
containing Restricted data must be shredded when no longer needed.

Data Retention
Customer data is retained for the duration of the contract plus 3 years, unless legal hold
applies. Employee records are retained for 7 years post-employment. Marketing data is reviewed
annually and purged if no longer needed.

Breach Response
Any suspected data breach must be reported to the Information Security team within 1 hour of
discovery. The IS team initiates the Incident Response Plan and notifies affected parties
within 72 hours as required by GDPR.
